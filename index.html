H&M scandal

Famous fashion company H&M came under fire in January 2018 for an ad that featured a black child wearing a sweatshirt with the words “coolest monkey in the jungle.” The ad caused public outrage over social media because of its racist content. The #coolestmonkeyinthejungle has stormed the internet, drawing widespread public accusation and debate. By observing the social phenomena caused by this scandal, it is clear that the world has entered a new age of media activism. But what does it mean?

(Matt, 2018)

"Media activism"

Media activism describes many types of activism that use media; activism based on video, audio, and websites are all types of media activism. Yet, social media remains the most vital form of media activism! The famous social event Black Lives Matter is the most outstanding example of effective media activism in recent decades.

Black Lives Matter

(Chaudhary, 2020)

To fight for the rights of African minorities, Black Lives Matter shocked the world in 2020. The graph on the right shows people’s sentiment towards the Black Lives Matter movement after gathering around 1000 tweets from Twitter in 2020. Unsurprisingly, most tweets appear neutral to the topic, yet the 28.3 percent positive feedback shows that people still support the cause behind this movement.

BLM impact

The data underneath demonstrates a close look at the Google query numbers relating to the Black Lives Matter movement. The number of queries skyrocketed in 2020 after the police murder of George Floyd. Despite the fact this movement existed as early as 2013, the 2020 protests achieved unparalleled success due to the use of media activism. Therefore, BLM remains the most potent demonstration of media activism, as social platforms can change the political and social environment. Civilians now have the power to unite against government or corporate organizations that have committed immoral crimes against the people. Media activism for the right cause can be considered the positive aspect of this phenomenon, but what about the negative? Media activism is the new reality, and its popularity and presence continue to grow with the popularity of social platforms.

(Google, n.d.)

"Possibility of Cyberbullying?"

It is also essential to look at the possibility of cyberbullying caused by media activism. The graph on the here is based on the comments gathered from Twitter and Facebook in 2021, the two most popular platforms for media activism. By observing the ratio between abusive and average tweets, it is frightening to see the number of abusive tweets approaching the usual comments. The proactive nature of media activism might result in extremist ideas, and innocent civilians might become the target of attack. It is essential to find ways to protect the safety of the innocent while the popularity of media activism grows. But even so, media activism still serves its purpose; the H&M incident remains an example of this.

"Media activism helps expose real-life industry exploitation!"

It is essential to know the outrage behind the H&M scandal is based on a real-life tragedy in which H&M has participated. H&M is among the top 20 most profitable fashion companies in the globe. Yet, there has been proof that H&M is participating in labor abuse happening in Asia and Africa. Workers in H&M have revealed labor law violations and cheap labor in the H&M supply chain (Campaign, 2018). Therefore, for H&M to use “coolest monkey in the jungle” in their ad goes beyond racial discrimination. It helped more people realize the corporate exploitation of ethnic minorities! “Fashion industry benefits from cheap labor of ethnic minorities!” The graph on the right-hand side shows the global purchase power of 100 countries in 2023. Africa and Asia show a dark red, meaning ethnic minorities are still suffering from low income and extreme poverty. Under such circumstances, H&M shows extreme racial insensitivity, considering how much profit they have already exploited from ethnic minorities.

(Magsi, 2023)

“Why you can make a difference.”

This graph shows the yearly operating profit of H&M. The number shows a clear drop in 2018, the year of media activism for the H&M scandal.

(Smith, 2024)

However, this isn’t the only data that shows the financial consequences of H&M caused by media activism. This graph shows the yearly global brand value of H&M; the brand demonstrates an apparent decline in global value after the 2018 incident.

(Sabanoglu, 2023)

Finally, the last graph shows the sales numbers of H&M in SEK. H&M suffered a financial hit in 2017 and 2018. The public shaming caused by media activism has upset the economic status of the fashion giant. Soon after the incident, H&M apologized for their offensive ad regarding ethnic minorities.

(Smith, 2024)

“Together, we can stop industry exploitation!”

What is clear is that media activism works; we have entered a time where social media has the power to change our lives for good. H&M has already faced its consequences, but many other corporations are continuing their works of oppression.
